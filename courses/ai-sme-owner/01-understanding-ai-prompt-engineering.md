## AI Capabilities & Limitations

### What AI Can & Cannot Do - Complete Overview

What AI Can Do (Today):
- Generate content quickly: Emails, ads, reports, product descriptions, social posts
- Organize and summarize information: Customer feedback, meeting notes, research, competitive analysis
- Assist with marketing: SEO ideas, copywriting, customer personas, social media planning
- Boost customer support: Auto-reply suggestions, chatbot scripts, FAQ creation, response templates
- Support decision-making: Generate options, different perspectives, spot blind spots, provide context
- Save time and improve efficiency: Small teams accomplish what previously required large teams

What AI Can't Do (Yet):
- Make decisions for you: It can give suggestions, but you still call the shots
- Replace your business instincts: AI has no context, no lived experience, no gut feel
- Understand your business deeply out of the box: You must provide clear prompts, context, and goals
- Handle sensitive judgment calls: HR issues, pricing decisions, high-stakes negotiations still your domain
- Run without supervision: AI needs guidance, refinement, oversight — it's a copilot, not autopilot

Bonus Tip: The better the input you give AI, the better the output you'll get. Learning to prompt is a business skill.

## AI Plus Prompt System Framework

### The Key to AI Success

Core Principle: Quality Instructions = Quality Results. Like training a new employee — clear instructions get great results, vague directions lead to confusion. This is the most important skill you'll learn today.

### AI Plus Prompt System - The 5 Pillars

1. GOAL: Clearly state what you want the AI to do — the main action or objective
2. USER ROLE: Tell the AI who it's pretending to be and who it's talking to
3. INSTRUCTIONS: Specify how the output should be structured — bullets, table, paragraphs
4. DETAILS: Additional information or boundaries such as word limits, tone rules and what to avoid
5. EXAMPLES: Give examples or references to guide tone, style or content structure

### Step 1 - GOAL

Goal: To articulate the desired end-product with absolute clarity. Use Strong, Specific Action Verbs. Specify the End Goal. Instead of: "Help with lunch business." Use: "Create simple lunch promotion strategy to bring back customers and increase lunch revenue by 30% within 6 weeks." Key Message: Clear specifications = usable output.

### Step 2 - USER ROLE

Goal: To move beyond generic information and create rich, contextual environment. Assign a Persona: "Act as a business consultant for Singapore heartland F&B owners." Provide Core Domain Knowledge: "I run Ah Seng's Zi Char in Ang Mo Kio with 10 tables. Average customer spend S$22 per person." Establish Situational Awareness: "Current problem: Lunch crowd dropped 20% since Circuit Breaker." Key Message: The more context, the better the results.

### Step 3 - INSTRUCTIONS

Goal: To dictate the exact structure, layout, and delivery format. Declare the Structure Explicitly. Request Specific Formatting Elements. Example: "Give me: 1. Quick Summary 2. 3 Simple Lunch Promotions 3. How to Tell Customers 4. Week-by-week Action Plan." Key Message: Format outputs can affect future workflows.

### Step 4 - DETAILS

Goal: To guide the AI by explicitly stating what it should not do and setting boundaries. Impose Negative Constraints: "Keep all promotions above S$15." Set Scope and Length Limits: "Must work with current kitchen setup." Examples: "No fancy ingredients we don't stock", "Use simple English, not too cheem." Key Message: This ensures AI stays on-brand and practical.

### Step 5 - EXAMPLES

Goal: To guide the AI's style, tone, quality, and structure by showing exactly what "good" looks like. Provide Clear Input/Output Pairs. Show Quality Standards. Example promotion style and WhatsApp message style. Key Message: This ensures output aligned with your preferences.
